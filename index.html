Other Factors Affecting Happiness

When it comes to happiness, it's not just about crime. Other important things include:

Freedom to Make Choices: People are happier when they can decide how they want to live their lives.

Corruption Perceptions: Trust in government and institutions affects happiness. Less corruption means more happiness.

Social Support: Having friends and family who care about you makes a big difference in how happy you feel.

These factors show that happiness is about more than just having a good income. It's also about freedom, trust, and the people in your life.

Impact of GDP and Life Expectancy on Happiness

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita serves as a measure of economic prosperity and access to resources within a country.
Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have better infrastructure, education, and healthcare, all of which contribute to an improved quality of life.

Life expectancy is a key indicator of a nation's overall health and well-being.
Longer life expectancy often correlates with a better healthcare system, lifestyle, and social support, all of which are linked to higher levels of happiness.

As we can see from this graph that plots GDP per capita and Life Expectancy, we can observe that the lower the GDP per capita and
life Expectancy, the lower the levels of happiness.

The countries with overall lower GDP + Life Expectancy rank around an average of 3 to 4 on the happiness score, while
the countries with overall higher GDP + Life Expectancy rank around an average of 6 to 7 on the happiness score.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while crime undeniably affects happiness by instilling fear and insecurity, it is just one piece of a complex puzzle. Economic prosperity, good health, freedom, trust, and social connections all play vital roles in shaping a happier society. Addressing and improving these interconnected aspects holistically is key to fostering overall well-being and happiness in communities.
